# Legal Guardrails (Plain English)

This is not legal advice.

This is a practical safety rail so owners and operators do not make avoidable legal mistakes while trying to stay contract-eligible.

---

## First Principle

**Do not claim what you cannot prove.**

If you affirm readiness, submit a score, or sign a clause, assume someone may ask for evidence later.

If you cannot produce it, you have risk.

---

## The 5 Guardrails

### Guardrail 1: Read the Clauses Before You Sign

At minimum, confirm whether these apply to your scope:

- FAR 52.204-21
- DFARS 252.204-7012
- DFARS 252.204-7021

If you do not know what they mean, pause and get clarification in writing.

### Guardrail 2: Treat Affirmations as Legal Statements

If you submit an SPRS related affirmation or self assessment posture, treat it like sworn business representation.

Do not submit based on hope.
Submit based on evidence.

### Guardrail 3: Keep Evidence With Dates

Undated screenshots and verbal claims are weak.

Keep dated:
- Account reviews
- Patch logs
- MFA status proof
- Encryption status proof
- Incident records

### Guardrail 4: Do Not Hide Known Gaps

A documented gap with a real remediation plan is far safer than pretending a control exists.

Use POA&M style tracking for known gaps:
- What is missing
- Risk level
- Owner
- Target date

### Guardrail 5: Report Incidents on Time

If your contract path requires incident reporting timelines, treat those as hard deadlines.

Under Rev 3-style expectations, 1-hour reporting windows can apply. "We'll handle it Monday" is not a safe assumption.

---

## Risky Statements to Stop Saying

- "We're compliant enough"
- "We'll fix that after award"
- "The consultant said we're good"
- "It is just boilerplate"
- "No one will ask for that evidence"

Replace with:

- "Here is the control, owner, and evidence"
- "Here is the open gap and target remediation date"

---

## Permission Boundaries for Scripts and Scans

Before running scans or scripts:

- Confirm you are authorized for the systems in scope
- Avoid scanning third party systems without written permission
- Log what was run, when, and by whom
- Keep outputs as evidence

Unauthorized scanning can create legal and contractual issues even if your intent is good.

---

## Before You Click "Affirm" Checklist

Only affirm when all are true:

- We know which clauses apply
- We know our level path
- We have evidence for major implemented controls
- We have documented remaining gaps with owners/dates
- Leadership understands what is being represented

If any of these is false, hold and remediate first.

---

## Escalate When Needed

Escalate to legal counsel or contracting officer clarification when:

- Clause interpretation is unclear
- Prime responses are conflicting
- Data classification is not explicit
- Incident reporting obligations are uncertain

Early clarification is cheaper than post-incident explanation.

# Questions to Ask Your Prime (Copy/Paste Ready)

If you are a subcontractor and do not know whether CUI will flow to you, ask these questions in writing before you sign.

This protects you, clarifies scope, and ensures you are not walking into a False Claims Act trap by signing a 7012 clause you cannot satisfy.

---

## The Core 10 Questions for 2026

1. **Data Classification:** Will any Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) or Covered Defense Information (CDI) be shared with our company for this scope?
2. **Flow-down Clauses:** Which specific clauses are being flowed down to us: FAR 52.204-21, DFARS 252.204-7012, 7019, 7020, or 7021?
3. **CMMC Phase Path:** Since we are in CMMC Phase 1, is a Level 2 self-assessment in SPRS sufficient for award, or is this a "prioritized acquisition" requiring a C3PAO certification immediately?
4. **CUI Specified:** Is any of the CUI designated as "Specified" (e.g., ITAR, export controlled, or nuclear)?
5. **DD Form 254:** Is there a DD Form 254 or a Security Classification Guide (SCG) attached to this subcontract?
6. **Asset Scope:** Which specific systems (cloud, on-prem, mobile) are you expecting us to use to process or store contract data?
7. **Cloud Constraints:** Are there restrictions on using non-FedRAMP cloud tools (e.g., standard Slack, Dropbox, or Zoom)?
8. **Incident Reporting:** Under DFARS 7012, what is our specific reporting path if we detect a compromise? Do we report to you or directly to DIBNet?
9. **Audit Rights:** Under DFARS 7020, are we required to grant the DIBCAC (government) access to our facilities for a Level 2 assessment, or is your C3PAO sufficient?
10. **Pre-Bid "Eyes-On":** Will our team be required to view sensitive equipment or secure areas during the site visit that requires a cleared escort or specific handling?

> [!TIP]
> The 2026 "Eyes-On" Trap: CUI risk is not just in files. Auditors are failing contractors whose field crews saw controlled technical data (CTD) on a shop floor during a walk-through without having a Level 2 baseline already in place. Ask Question 10 before you send a single person to a job site. If your crew sees sensitive equipment, that observation becomes part of your CUI scope whether you planned for it or not.

## Short Email Template (Fast Version)

Subject: Cyber Clause and Data Handling Clarification — [Project Name]

Hello [Prime POC],

To align our technical execution and ensure our SPRS posture is current for this award, please confirm:

- Whether any CUI/CDI will flow to our company for this scope
- The specific list of flow-down clauses (specifically 7012, 7019, and 7021)
- Whether a Level 2 self-assessment is sufficient or if a C3PAO certification is required for award
- Whether a DD Form 254 applies to our portion of the work

We want to confirm these requirements before we finalize our technical baseline and staffing.

Thank you,
[Your Name]
[Company]

## Detailed Email Template (When Scope Is Complex)

Subject: Request for Written Security and Data Handling Clarification

Hello [Prime POC],

We are preparing our internal technical and evidence baseline for this effort and need written clarification on data handling and flow down requirements.

Please confirm:

1. Data classification
- Will we receive, process, store, or transmit any CUI?
- If yes, what CUI categories are expected?

2. Clause flow down
- Does FAR 52.204-21 apply to our subcontract scope?
- Does DFARS 252.204-7012 apply to our subcontract scope?
- Does DFARS 252.204-7021 apply to our subcontract scope?

3. Assessment path
- Is CMMC third party assessment required for this subcontract scope?
- If self assessment is permitted, what affirmation/reporting timeline applies?

4. Attachments and handling constraints
- Is there a DD Form 254 or equivalent security attachment?
- Are there restrictions on cloud tools, remote access, or personal devices?
- What incident reporting timeline and process applies?
- Will we be required to access secure areas or view sensitive equipment during the pre bid site visit that requires specific handling or a cleared escort?

Please provide responses in writing so we can align controls, staffing, and evidence collection before performance begins.

Thank you,
[Your Name]
[Company]

## If They Give You a Vague Answer

If the answer is "probably no CUI" or "we'll let you know later," respond with this:

"Understood. For risk management, please confirm in writing whether CUI will or will not flow to our company for this scope, and which clauses apply to us."

If they still avoid a direct answer, treat that as risk and build conservative.

---

## Red Flags to Watch for in 2026

- **"We'll identify the CUI after award."** This is a trap. They know CUI is coming but do not want to pay for your compliance setup yet. Treat as high risk.
- **"Do not worry about the 7012 clause; it is just boilerplate."** Boilerplate clauses still carry False Claims Act liability. Never ignore them.
- **"Just use your personal email for now to keep things moving."** This is an immediate data spill and a compliance violation. Do not do this.
- **"We do not know if you need a C3PAO yet."** If the contract includes 7021 and CUI, assume you need one or at minimum a perfect 110/110 self-score in SPRS.
- **"You can affirm SPRS without a formal SSP."** You cannot. An SSP is the evidence artifact. Affirming without one is a False Claims Act violation.

---

## What "Good" Looks Like

A prime contractor that understands the Phase 1 rollout will provide:

- A clear written "Yes" or "No" on CUI flow (not "probably no")
- A list of CUI categories (e.g., CTI, procurement info, technical data)
- Written confirmation of the SPRS scoring requirements or C3PAO path
- A named security point of contact (not just the procurement buyer)
- A DD Form 254 or security attachment, or a written statement that none applies
- Incident reporting procedures in writing
- Clarity on whether the prime's assessment covers you or you need your own

If you get this early, your project is safer, your bid is stronger, and your compliance timeline is realistic.

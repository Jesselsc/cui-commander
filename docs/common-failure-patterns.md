# Common Failure Patterns (How Contractors Actually Get Burned)

Most companies do not fail because they are lazy.

They fail because they make normal business assumptions in a compliance environment where assumptions cost contracts.

Use this page as a reality check.

---

## Failure Pattern 1: "We have good IT, so we are fine"

What happens:
- Systems look decent
- No clean evidence trail
- Audit asks for proof, not stories

Why it fails:
- Auditors grade evidence quality, not confidence level

Fix:
- Keep an SSP draft current
- Keep account, patch, and access logs current
- Save screenshots/reports as dated evidence

---

## Failure Pattern 2: Affirming SPRS Without Backing Evidence

What happens:
- Team enters a score to look competitive
- Evidence package is incomplete

Why it fails:
- Legal exposure under false statement frameworks
- Prime/government confidence drops fast when challenged

Fix:
- Do not affirm what you cannot prove
- Treat affirmation as a legal checkpoint, not a sales step

---

## Failure Pattern 3: "We are just a sub, this doesn't apply"

What happens:
- Subcontract signed
- Flow down clauses ignored

Why it fails:
- CUI obligations can flow to subs directly
- Contract language, not job title, controls

Fix:
- Read flow downs before signature
- Ask prime for written confirmation of CUI and clause applicability

---

## Failure Pattern 4: Flat Network, No Real Segmentation

What happens:
- Office devices, guest devices, and sensitive systems share the same lane

Why it fails:
- One compromised endpoint can expose everything
- Boundary enforcement evidence is weak

Fix:
- Separate guest, office, and sensitive zones
- Document firewall rules and network map

---

## Failure Pattern 5: MFA Is Partial

What happens:
- MFA enabled on some accounts, not all admin paths
- Legacy admin accounts left behind

Why it fails:
- Attackers use weakest account path
- Assessments look for complete admin protection

Fix:
- Inventory every admin account
- Enforce MFA on all privileged accounts
- Prefer phishing-resistant options where possible

---

## Failure Pattern 6: Stale Accounts Stay Active

What happens:
- Former employees or old contractors still have access

Why it fails:
- Basic account governance failure
- Easy finding for assessors

Fix:
- Monthly account review
- Disable unused accounts quickly
- Keep a dated account review log

---

## Failure Pattern 7: Patch Work Is Ad Hoc

What happens:
- Updates happen "when there is time"
- No consistent schedule or evidence

Why it fails:
- Drift over time
- No proof of repeatable process

Fix:
- Enable automatic updates where possible
- Keep monthly patch log
- Run periodic vulnerability scans

---

## Failure Pattern 8: Contract Clauses Read Too Late

What happens:
- Team starts work before understanding clause obligations

Why it fails:
- Wrong baseline built
- Remediation becomes expensive and rushed

Fix:
- Read clauses at bid stage
- Confirm FAR/DFARS applicability in writing

---

## Failure Pattern 9: Treating Policies as "Optional Paperwork"

What happens:
- Technical controls exist, policies are weak or missing

Why it fails:
- Evidence package is incomplete
- Governance controls cannot be demonstrated

Fix:
- Keep lightweight, honest policy set
- Tie each policy to actual practice and evidence

---

## Failure Pattern 10: Waiting Too Long for Assessment Readiness

What happens:
- Team delays readiness work until contract pressure is high

Why it fails:
- C3PAO scheduling + remediation windows are long
- Rushed fixes break operations and evidence quality

Fix:
- Start baseline early
- Validate quarterly, not once

---

## The Fast Self-Check (10 Minutes)

If you answer "no" to any of these, you have a real gap:

- Can we prove MFA on every admin path?
- Can we show account review records for the last 30-60 days?
- Can we show patch and vulnerability evidence from this quarter?
- Can we point to the exact clauses that apply to us?
- Can we back every major claim in our SSP with evidence?

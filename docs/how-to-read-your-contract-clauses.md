# How to Read Your Contract Clauses (Without Losing Your Mind)

This page is for owners and project leads who are staring at a solicitation or subcontract and thinking: "Where do I even look for CMMC requirements?"

You do not need to be a lawyer to do this first pass.

You only need to answer one question:

**Will this contract make us handle FCI only, or CUI too?**

That answer decides your level.

---

## The 10-Minute Method

Use this in order.

1. Find the clause sections in the solicitation or subcontract.
2. Search for specific clause numbers.
3. Check whether a DD Form 254 is attached.
4. Decide likely level.
5. Send a written clarification question if anything is unclear.

---

## Step 1: Where to Look

In most federal solicitations, start with:

- Section H (Special Contract Requirements)
- Section I (Contract Clauses)
- Section J (Attachments / exhibits)

In most subcontracts, look for:

- "Flow-Down Clauses"
- "Cybersecurity"
- "Data Rights"
- "Information Security"
- "Compliance Requirements"

If your file is a PDF, use search and type each clause number exactly.

> [!WARNING]
> Primes often include one sentence like: "All FAR/DFARS clauses from the prime contract are incorporated by reference."
> This is the "Everything Bagel" of risk. If you see that sentence, do not assume the one page PO is the full requirement set. Use the Copy/Paste Clarification Question below immediately.

---

## Step 2: The Clause Numbers That Matter First

### FAR 52.204-21

What it means in plain English:
- Basic safeguarding for federal contract information (FCI)
- This is the Level 1 world

What to do:
- Build Level 1 baseline
- Keep evidence clean

### DFARS 252.204-7012

What it means in plain English:
- You are in DoD CUI handling territory
- NIST 800-171 obligations are in play

What to do:
- Plan for Level 2 controls and evidence
- Do not sign and ignore

### DFARS 252.204-7021

What it means in plain English:
- CMMC requirement clause is in the contract path
- Third party assessment may be required depending on program

What to do:
- Confirm assessment path and timeline early
- Start readiness now, not after award

---

## Step 3: Check for DD Form 254

If there is a DD Form 254 (Contract Security Classification Specification), read it.

Why it matters:
- It describes security handling requirements for the program
- It is often the clearest signal that controlled information is involved

If the prime mentions a DD254 but does not provide it, ask for the relevant handling requirements in writing.

---

## Quick Decision Guide

Use this as a first pass:

- FAR 52.204-21 only, no CUI language: likely Level 1
- DFARS 252.204-7012 present: treat as Level 2 path
- DFARS 252.204-7021 present: CMMC path is active, confirm level and assessment type
- Controlled drawings/specs/data in scope, even if clause text is messy: likely Level 2

Contract language always controls. When unclear, ask in writing.

## Copy/Paste Clarification Question (To Contracting Officer or Prime)

Subject: Clarification Request - Information Handling and Cyber Clauses

Hello [Name],

Before we finalize our approach and scope, please confirm the following for this requirement:

1. Will our company receive, store, process, or transmit any CUI as part of performance?
2. Does this contract include FAR 52.204-21?
3. Does this contract include DFARS 252.204-7012 and/or DFARS 252.204-7021 as applicable flow down clauses?
4. Is there a DD Form 254 or equivalent security handling attachment tied to this requirement?

We are confirming this early so we build the correct technical and evidence baseline before performance.

Thank you,
[Your Name]
[Company]

---

## Mistakes That Cost People Contracts

- Assuming "we are just a sub" means no CUI obligations
- Signing a subcontract before reading flow down clauses
- Treating CUI language like optional paperwork
- Waiting until award to ask what data is in scope
